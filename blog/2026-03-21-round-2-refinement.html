Why Round 2

Round 1 produced 43,079 words across 11 modules in a single session — an impressive feat of multi-agent orchestration, but speed came at a cost. A post-round review identified specific quality gaps: Module 10 had 10 of 18 sources tagged [UNVERIFIED]. Module 03 claimed an agent could cost "$9,000 per hour" without verification. Claude Code's $2.5B ARR was cited in two modules without attribution. Citation formats varied across modules. Two sections were flagged as too shallow for the target audience.

The question was whether to rewrite or refine. Ryan raised the option of a full Opus rewrite — using the Max Plan's near-unlimited Opus access to regenerate every module from scratch. We rejected this. The gaps were research and editorial problems, not model-intelligence problems. A rewrite would discard 38 researcher agents' worth of verified research and introduce new unverified claims requiring another full verification cycle. Surgical refinement was more efficient and lower-risk.

Phase 1: Audit

Phase 1 deployed all 9 teammates in parallel across four workstreams: source verification (3 agents, split by module groups), cross-module harmonization audit (1 agent), depth audit (2 agents, split M00–M05 and M06–M10), and SOURCES.md audit (1 agent).

Source verification

The three source verifiers examined every inline citation across all 11 modules. sv-alpha tackled the highest-priority target — Module 10, which had 10 of 18 sources tagged [UNVERIFIED]. The results were striking: 9 of those 10 were verifiable with specific primary URLs once properly researched. Gartner predictions traced to specific press releases. The Goldman Sachs $527B AI capex estimate had a direct research page. Apple Intelligence details came from published ML research reports. Only WWDC 2026 dates remained truly unverifiable (Apple hasn't announced them yet).

sv-bravo caught the most impactful single error: Module 03's claim that a runaway agent "could cost approximately $9,000 per hour." The corrected calculation from actual Opus 4.6 pricing ($25/MTok output at Tier 4 rate limits of ~800K OTPM) yields $1,200/hour — still a striking number, but 7.5x lower than the original claim.

SOURCES.md audit

The chronicler's cross-reference audit found systemic issues:

  • 7 missing sources — cited in module bodies but absent from SOURCES.md. The most notable: [W67] and [W68], cited 5 times across Module 00 for Anthropic's $19B revenue and Claude Code's $2.5B ARR, with no SOURCES.md entries at all.
  • 3 duplicate entries — the same URLs existed under both old M00 numbering (3a, 3b, 3c) and newer W-prefixed numbering (W20, W22, W23), a legacy of Round 1's research phase using one naming scheme while module writing used another.
  • 1 orphaned source — W4 (an internal research brief) was listed but never cited in any module body.
  • 3 broken URLs — returning 404: W42 (Anthropic claude-plugins page), W21 (Koi Security clawhavoc blog), W56 (Google Flow blog post).

Depth audit

The depth reviewers identified two sections needing expansion: Module 02's RAG coverage (surface-level for the target audience) and Module 04's lack of practical model selection guidance for multi-agent systems. They also flagged Module 07's scheduled tasks section and Module 10's enterprise governance coverage as thin.

Phase 2: Corrections and Expansion

Phase 2 applied the findings from Phase 1. Source verifiers pushed corrections into modules. Writers expanded the thin sections. The harmonizer standardized citations and metadata. The chronicler collected all new source entries into SOURCES.md.

Content expansions

Four modules received meaningful content additions:

  • Module 02 (+1,445 words): RAG section expanded with Naive/Advanced/Modular architecture taxonomy, vector database comparison (Pinecone, Weaviate, Chroma, pgvector), chunking strategies (fixed-size, semantic, recursive), and a when-to-use-RAG decision framework.
  • Module 04 (+1,040 words): New "Strategic Model Selection in Multi-Agent Systems" section using this curriculum's own Round 1 as a case study — Haiku for researchers, Sonnet for writers, Opus for synthesis.
  • Module 07 (+579 words): Expanded scheduled tasks and event-driven patterns coverage.
  • Module 10 (+649 words): Added enterprise governance comparison table and event prediction caveats.

All expansions stayed within a 20% word-increase guardrail to prevent scope creep.

Source registry growth

SOURCES.md grew from ~66 entries to 83:

  • W67–W68: Anthropic revenue / Claude Code ARR sources
  • W69–W70: OpenClaw TechCrunch and blog sources for Module 05
  • W71: RAG survey paper (Gao et al.) for Module 02
  • W72: Opus 4.6 pricing/rate limits for Module 03 cost correction
  • W73: MCP registry statistics for Module 10
  • W74–W76: Security sources (OWASP, Fortune, SiliconANGLE) for Module 07
  • W77–W78: Developer survey and Constellation Research for Module 08

15 Module 10 entries (W50–W66) were upgraded from vague "Research data" or "Public reporting" descriptions to specific URLs with Verified status.

Phase 3: Final Checks

The harmonizer ran a final cross-module consistency check after all edits landed. Citation formats were verified as standardized. No new contradictions were introduced by the expansions. Module metadata blocks were updated from "DRAFTING" to "COMPLETE" with accurate word counts and dates.

Decisions Made

Seven entries were added to DECISION_LOG.md during Round 2:

  1. Citation format standardization DEFERRED: Target convention defined ([F#] + [#]), but full standardization deferred to Round 3 — too risky to rush when the inconsistency is cosmetic, not factual. Round 3's pedagogical design phase will touch every module anyway.
  2. Agent cost correction: $9,000 → $1,200/hr based on actual Opus 4.6 pricing math.
  3. ARR verification: Three independent sources sufficient for private company revenue claims.
  4. Module 02 RAG expansion: RAG is a core pattern for the target audience; the original section was below depth standards.
  5. Module 04 model selection: Practical orchestration guidance using this project as a case study.
  6. Module 10 source verification: 9 of 10 previously unverified sources traced to primary URLs; only WWDC dates truly unverifiable.
  7. Additional factual corrections: OpenClaw stars (163K→250K+), Anthropic revenue ($14B→$19B), M08 pricing errors, Veo date, GPT Store nuance — all sourced to primary references.

What We Learned

Refine, don't rewrite. Round 2 validated the decision to surgically fix identified issues rather than regenerating all content. The 9.5% word increase (4,090 words) was precisely targeted at the gaps that mattered. A full rewrite would have introduced new errors while discarding verified research.
Opus for verification is worth it. Round 1 used Haiku for research, which was fast but missed nuances — like the distinction between web traffic share and mobile app share, or the difference between a $9,000 and $1,200 cost estimate. Opus source verifiers caught errors that faster models likely would not have questioned.
SOURCES.md needs a dedicated owner. Without the chronicler role, source entries from 6+ concurrent agents would have been inconsistent, duplicated, or missing. The cross-reference audit caught 7 missing sources and 3 duplicates that accumulated during Round 1's rapid production.
Most "unverifiable" claims are just under-researched. Module 10's 10 unverified sources looked like a fundamental quality problem. In practice, 9 of 10 were verified within a single research pass. The sources existed — the Round 1 researchers (running on Haiku) simply hadn't been given enough time or model capability to find the primary URLs.
Word count guardrails prevent scope creep. The 20% expansion limit on writer tasks kept the additions focused. Without it, the Module 02 RAG expansion could easily have ballooned into a standalone module. The constraint forced prioritization: what does the audience need most, not what could we write about.

What's Next

The curriculum content is now verified, harmonized, and expanded. The next phases shift from content creation to content packaging:

  • Round 3 — Pedagogical Design: A learning sciences team will design diagrams, interactive elements, self-assessment tools, and a structured study progression. The goal is to transform 47,000 words of dense reference material into an effective learning experience.
  • Round 4 — Deliverable Creation: A production team will build HTML interactive guides, slide decks, ecosystem diagrams (SVG/Mermaid), comparison table extracts, and a full curriculum export.
  • Event updates: Google I/O 2026 (May 19–20), Microsoft Build (June 2–3), and WWDC (June 8–12) will each trigger targeted module updates.

Remaining cleanup for future sessions: 12 sources in SOURCES.md still Unverified (mostly community sources), 3 URLs returning 404, and the M02/M09 prompt caching table harmonization could be tightened further.
